# Manas Prakriti -- Quiz Logic and Scoring System

## Scientific Foundation

This assessment is based on the PhD thesis **"Manasa Vijnana"** by **Akolkar Prasad Pramod** (University of Mumbai, 2019). The primary reference text is the **Astanga Hrdayam of Vagbhata**, one of the foundational treatises of Ayurveda.

The core concept: every human mind contains three fundamental qualities -- **Sattva**, **Rajas**, and **Tamas** -- in a unique proportion. This proportion constitutes one's **Manas Prakriti** (mental constitution). Unlike physical constitution (Sharira Prakriti), Manas Prakriti specifically describes the psychological and behavioral tendencies of an individual.

---

## The Three Gunas

### Sattva -- Purity and Calm

**Nature**: Sattva represents clarity, harmony, purity, and balance. It is the quality of light, awareness, and inner peace.

**Associated Dosha**: Kapha (stability, nurturing)

**Mental Characteristics (Bhavas)**:
- **Shaucha** -- purity, cleanliness of mind and environment
- **Astikya** -- faith in karma and moral order
- **Shukla-dharma-ruci** -- inclination toward righteous conduct
- **Smriti** -- strong memory and retention
- **Titiksha** -- forbearance, ability to endure hardship
- **Ahimsa** -- compassion, non-violence
- **Satya** -- truthfulness
- **Dharma** -- righteousness, duty
- **Kshama** -- patience, forgiveness
- **Achara** -- discipline, good conduct
- **Anahamkara** -- humility, absence of ego
- **Medha** -- intellect and retention
- **Tyaga** -- selfless service, renunciation

**Behavioral Patterns**: Truthful, patient, compassionate, devoted, good memory, disciplined, humble, environmentally conscious, prefers righteous action over personal gain.

### Rajas -- Activity and Passion

**Nature**: Rajas represents energy, activity, ambition, and dynamism. It is the driving force behind all action and creation, but also behind restlessness, desire, and agitation.

**Associated Dosha**: Pitta (transformation, intensity)

**Mental Characteristics (Bhavas)**:
- **Bahubhashittva** -- talkativeness, expressiveness
- **Mana** -- pride in achievement
- **Krodha** -- anger, irritability
- **Dambha** -- arrogance, pretense
- **Matsarya** -- jealousy, envy
- **Amarsha** -- impatience
- **Ahamkara** -- ego expression
- **Aishwarya** -- material desire, acquisitiveness
- **Spardha** -- competitiveness
- **Shathya** -- cunning, taking shortcuts
- **Mada** -- stubbornness, insistence
- **Vitatha** -- exaggeration
- **Krodha-bahya** -- outward aggression

**Behavioral Patterns**: Ambitious, impatient, proud, competitive, expressive, acquisitive, prone to anger when frustrated, prefers shortcuts, exaggerates experiences.

### Tamas -- Inertia and Grounding

**Nature**: Tamas represents inertia, darkness, heaviness, and grounding. It provides stability and rest, but in excess leads to laziness, ignorance, and resistance to change.

**Associated Dosha**: Vata (irregularity, instability -- paradoxically, tamasic inertia can manifest as vata-like avoidance)

**Mental Characteristics (Bhavas)**:
- **Bhaya** -- fear, anxiety
- **Ajnana** -- ignorance, disinterest in learning
- **Nidra** -- excessive sleep, drowsiness
- **Vishada** -- depression, dejection
- **Akarmashilata** -- aversion to action
- **Alasya** -- laziness, procrastination
- **Amedha** -- poor intellect, disinterest in new skills
- **Buddhi-nasha** -- avoidance of mental effort
- **Smriti-nasha** -- forgetfulness

**Behavioral Patterns**: Inert, forgetful, avoidant, resistant to change, prefers sleeping, misses deadlines, avoids responsibility, dislikes learning new things.

### Answer Options

Each question has three possible answers:
- **YES** -- the characteristic described is consistently present
- **NO** -- the characteristic is not present
- **SOMETIMES** -- the characteristic is occasionally present

## Scoring Algorithm

### Step 1: Raw Count

Count YES, NO, and SOMETIMES answers per section.

**Example**:
- Sattva: 7 YES, 1 NO, 2 SOMETIMES
- Rajas: 4 YES, 3 NO, 3 SOMETIMES
- Tamas: 0 YES, 4 NO, 1 SOMETIMES

### Step 2: Primary Percentage

Calculate YES count divided by section total, times 100.

```
Primary % = (YES count / section total) * 100
```

**Example**:
- Sattva: 7 / 10 = **70.00%**
- Rajas: 4 / 10 = **40.00%**
- Tamas: 0 / 5 = **0.00%**

### Step 3: SOMETIMES Conversion (Thesis-Defined Ratios)

This is the key insight from the thesis. The SOMETIMES answer is not treated as a neutral 50/50 split. Instead, the conversion ratio depends on the nature of the guna:

**Sattva and Tamas (dormant gunas): SOMETIMES -> 33% YES, 67% NO**

Rationale: Sattvika and tamasika qualities are dormant or inactive by nature. Sattva is a state of equilibrium that requires sustained effort. Tamas is inertia that only manifests when it fully takes hold. In both cases, a "sometimes" response suggests the quality is not strongly present -- the individual leans toward inaction or non-expression. Therefore, only 33% of SOMETIMES converts to YES.

**Rajas (active guna): SOMETIMES -> 67% YES, 33% NO**

Rationale: Rajas is the driving force behind all activity. It is inherently expressive and outward-facing. A "sometimes" response for a rajasic quality means the tendency is there but not always expressed -- yet the underlying drive exists. Because rajas by nature leans toward action, 67% of SOMETIMES converts to YES.

**Example** (continuing from above):
```
Sattva adjusted YES = 7 + (2 * 0.33) = 7.66
Rajas adjusted YES  = 4 + (3 * 0.67) = 6.01
Tamas adjusted YES  = 0 + (1 * 0.33) = 0.33
```

### Step 4: Secondary Percentage

Calculate adjusted YES count divided by section total, times 100.

```
Secondary % = (adjusted YES count / section total) * 100
```

**Example**:
- Sattva: 7.66 / 10 * 100 = **76.60%**
- Rajas: 6.01 / 10 * 100 = **60.10%**
- Tamas: 0.33 / 5 * 100 = **6.60%**

### Step 5: Dominant Guna Classification

Sort all three gunas by secondary percentage in descending order:

```
Sattva (76.60%) > Rajas (60.10%) > Tamas (6.60%)
```

- **Primary dominant guna** = the highest scorer (Sattva)
- **Gap** = difference between #1 and #2 = 76.60 - 60.10 = **16.50%**
- If gap <= 10%: **dual type** (both gunas are co-dominant)
- If gap > 10%: **single type** (clear dominance)
- 16.50% > 10% -> **Single type: Sattvika**

Special case: if all three gunas are within 10% of each other -> **Trigunatmaka** (tri-guna balanced)

### Step 6: Prakriti Type

There are 7 possible prakriti types:

| Type | Condition | Archetype Title |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Sattvika | Sattva dominant, gap > 10% | The Harmoniser |
| Rajasika | Rajas dominant, gap > 10% | The Dynamo |
| Tamasika | Tamas dominant, gap > 10% | The Anchor |
| Sattva-Rajasika | Sattva highest + Rajas within 10% | The Enlightened Leader |
| Rajo-Sattvika | Rajas highest + Sattva within 10% | The Passionate Visionary |
| Sattva-Tamasika | Sattva highest + Tamas within 10% | The Reflective Sage |
| Rajo-Tamasika | Rajas highest + Tamas within 10% | The Restless Warrior |
| Trigunatmaka | All three within 10% of each other | The Balanced One |

Note: The naming convention places the primary dominant guna first. "Sattva-Rajasika" means Sattva leads but Rajas is close behind.

### Step 7: Sattva Bala (Mental Strength)

Sattva Bala is determined solely by the sattva secondary percentage. It measures the individual's mental resilience and clarity:

| Sattva Secondary % | Sattva Bala | Meaning |
|---------------------|-------------|---------|
| >= 66% | **Pravara** (Superior) | Strong mental resilience, clarity, and inner stability. The individual has robust psychological resources. |
| 33% -- 65% | **Madhya** (Moderate) | Average mental strength. Good baseline with room for growth through practice. |
| < 33% | **Avara** (Needs Strengthening) | Mental wellness requires focused attention. Practices to cultivate sattva are especially recommended. |

## 16 Sub-types (Deep Assessment)

The deep assessment (80 questions) enables classification into 16 sub-types from the thesis, mapped to mythological and natural archetypes:

### 7 Sattvika Sub-types

| Sub-type | Archetype | Key Traits (Anuka/Svabhava) |
|----------|-----------|---------------------------|
| Brahma kaya | The Creator | Supreme wisdom, truthfulness, mastery of senses, deep knowledge |
| Mahendra kaya | The Sovereign | Leadership, authority, valor, commanding presence |
| Varuna kaya | The Upholder | Justice, patience, righteous anger only for dharma, love of water/purity |
| Kaubera kaya | The Steward | Wealth management, generosity balanced with prudence, enjoyment of luxury with dharma |
| Gandharva kaya | The Artist | Love of music/dance/art, poetic nature, aesthetic sensibility |
| Yamya kaya | The Judge | Impartial judgment, timeliness, discipline, respect for rules |
| Rishi kaya | The Sage | Devotion to learning, austerity, meditation, spiritual practices |

### 6 Rajasika Sub-types

| Sub-type | Archetype | Key Traits |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Asura kaya | The Titan | Brave, authoritative, fearsome, proud, fond of display |
| Sarpa kaya | The Serpent | Sharp when provoked, lazy when comfortable, brave but vengeful |
| Shakuna kaya | The Hawk | Quick-tempered, attached to food/pleasure, restless |
| Rakshasa kaya | The Warrior | Fierce, intolerant, angry, enjoys conflict, strong appetite |
| Paishaca kaya | The Phantom | Gluttonous, loves secrets, unusual habits, nocturnal tendencies |
| Preta kaya | The Ghost | Excessive desire, envious, stingy, resentful, lack of self-care |

### 3 Tamasika Sub-types

| Sub-type | Archetype | Key Traits |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Pashu kaya | The Ox | Dull intellect, excessive eating/sleeping, fond of routine |
| Matsya kaya | The Fish | Timid, restless, unstable desires, fond of water, easily swayed |

Each sub-type is determined by pattern-matching specific bhava combinations in the deep assessment's 80 questions.

## Demographic Adjustments

Demographics are collected before the quiz and influence interpretation, not scoring.

### Age Weighting

Age contextualizes the guna scores within normal developmental patterns:

| Age Range | Context | Adjustment |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| 18-25 | Higher baseline Rajas is natural (ambition, exploration phase) | Rajas score adjusted down by ~5% for classification purposes |
| 26-45 | Peak Rajas-Sattva balance period | No adjustment |
| 46-55 | Natural shift toward Sattva (reflection, wisdom) | Slight Sattva normalization |
| 56+ | Natural increase in both Sattva (wisdom) and Tamas (physical slowing) | Expectations adjusted accordingly |

### Gender Context

- Gender is **not used** for score adjustment to avoid introducing bias into the classification.
- Gender **is used** for contextualizing AI recommendations -- different lifestyle suggestions, different cultural contexts, etc.

### Diet, Work Nature, Sleep Quality

These are **not used** in the scoring algorithm. They serve two purposes:

1. **AI insights personalization**: Fed into the LLM prompt so recommendations are specific and actionable (e.g., a desk worker with poor sleep gets different advice than a physical laborer with good sleep).

2. **Cross-validation**: Used to check consistency between self-reported behavior and quiz results. For example, poor sleep quality combined with high Tamas suggests a consistent pattern, while poor sleep with high Sattva might suggest external stressors rather than constitutional tendency.

## AI Personalization Layer

### Scoring is Deterministic

The scoring algorithm (Steps 1-7) involves zero AI or machine learning. It is a purely mathematical computation that produces identical results whether run on the backend (Python `ScoringEngine`) or the frontend (TypeScript `computeScoringResult`). This is intentional -- the assessment must be reproducible and explainable.

### AI is for Interpretation Only

AI is used exclusively for the interpretive text layer. After scoring is complete, the system generates personalized insights by sending a structured prompt to the selected LLM provider.

### Prompt Composition

The prompt includes:
- Prakriti type and archetype title
- Primary and secondary dominant gunas
- Sattva/Rajas/Tamas secondary percentages
- Sattva Bala grade
- Locale (determines response language: English, Hindi, or Marathi)
- Demographics: age, gender, diet, work nature, sleep quality

The prompt requests:
1. A warm 2-sentence explanation of what this prakriti means personally
2. One specific daily practice recommendation tailored to the guna balance
3. One encouraging thought about natural strengths

### Constraints
- Max 150 words
- Plain text (no markdown formatting)
- Warm, non-judgmental, empowering tone
- Simple language
- Multi-LLM support: the same prompt is sent to whichever provider is selected

## Validation

### Thesis Survey Data

The original thesis surveyed **210 participants**, yielding:
- 166 classified as **Sattvika-Rajasika** (Sattva-dominant with strong Rajas)
- 44 classified as **Rajasika-Sattvika** (Rajas-dominant with strong Sattva)

This distribution aligns with the general population tendency toward Sattva-Rajas dual types, validating the question design and scoring ratios.

### Unit Tests

The scoring engine has comprehensive test coverage (`tests/test_scoring_engine.py`):

- All-YES Sattva -> Sattvika, Pravara
- All-YES Rajas -> Rajasika, Avara sattva bala
- All-YES Tamas -> Tamasika
- Mixed high Sattva + Rajas (gap <= 10%) -> Sattva-Rajasika dual type
- SOMETIMES conversion with dormant ratio (Sattva: 33% YES)
- SOMETIMES conversion with active ratio (Rajas: 67% YES)
- Equal scores -> dual type classification
- Full assessment (30/30/20 totals)
- Sattva Bala: Pravara (>= 66%), Madhya (33-65%), Avara (< 33%)
- All-SOMETIMES answers -> correct conversion (Sattva 33%, Rajas 67%, Tamas 33%)

### Client-Server Parity

The scoring algorithm is implemented identically in:
- **Backend**: `app/services/scoring_engine.py` (Python, Decimal arithmetic)
- **Frontend**: `stores/assessment-store.ts` (TypeScript, floating-point arithmetic)

Both implementations use the same constants (0.33/0.67 ratios, 10% dual-type threshold), the same step sequence, and the same classification logic. The frontend uses the client-side result as a fallback when the backend is unreachable, and as a validation check when both are available.
